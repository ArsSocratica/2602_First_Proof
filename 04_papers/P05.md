# Problem 5 — The $\mathcal{O}$-Slice Filtration and a Geometric Fixed Point Characterization

## Theorem

Let $G$ be a finite group and $\mathcal{O}$ an $N_\infty$ operad with associated transfer system $\mathcal{T}$ and admissible family $\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}} = \{H \leq G : e \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} H\}$. Define the **$\mathcal{O}$-slice cells** to be the $G$-spectra $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H}$ ($n \geq 0$) and $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H - 1}$ ($n \geq 1$) for $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$, and say $X \in \mathrm{Sp}^G_{\mathcal{O}}$ is **$\mathcal{O}$-slice $\geq n$** if $[\hat{S}, \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^G = 0$ for every $\mathcal{O}$-slice cell $\hat{S}$ of dimension $< n$.

Then for connective $X \in \mathrm{Sp}^G_{\mathcal{O}}$:

$$X \text{ is } \mathcal{O}\text{-slice} \geq n \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \pi_k(\Phi^H X) = 0 \text{ for all } H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}} \text{ and all } k < \lfloor n/|H| \rfloor.$$

**Answer: YES** — the $\mathcal{O}$-slice filtration admits a geometric fixed point characterization, generalizing Hill–Yarnall [HY18, Theorem 3.1].

---

## Setup and Conventions

Let $G$ be a finite group and let $\mathcal{O}$ be an $N_\infty$ operad for $G$. By the classification theorem of Blumberg–Hill [BH15, Theorem 1.4], independently established by Rubin [Rub17, Theorem 1.1], Gutiérrez–White [GW17, Theorem 1.2], and Bonventre–Pereira [BP17, Theorem 1.6], the homotopy type of $\mathcal{O}$ is determined by its associated **transfer system** $\mathcal{T}$ on the poset $\mathrm{Sub}(G)$, satisfying:
1. **Reflexivity**: $H \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} H$ for all $H \leq G$.
2. **Transitivity**: If $K \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} H$ and $H \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} J$, then $K \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} J$.
3. **Conjugation invariance**: If $K \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} H$, then $gKg^{-1} \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} gHg^{-1}$ for all $g \in G$.
4. **Restriction**: If $K \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} H$ and $L \leq H$, then $(K \cap L) \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} L$.

A subgroup $H \leq G$ is **$\mathcal{O}$-admissible** if $e \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} H$. The family $\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$ always contains $e$, and equals $\mathrm{Sub}(G)$ when $\mathcal{O}$ is the terminal $E_\infty$ operad.

We denote by $\mathrm{Sp}^G$ the genuine $G$-equivariant stable category. Following [BH19, §4], $\mathcal{O}$ determines an **incomplete equivariant stable category** $\mathrm{Sp}^G_{\mathcal{O}}$ with canonical functor $\iota_{\mathcal{O}} : \mathrm{Sp}^G_{\mathcal{O}} \to \mathrm{Sp}^G$. For $H \leq G$, the **geometric fixed point functor** is $\Phi^H : \mathrm{Sp}^G \to \mathrm{Sp}$, and we write $\Phi^H_{\mathcal{O}} := \Phi^H \circ \iota_{\mathcal{O}}$.

---

## Definitions

**$\mathcal{O}$-slice cells.** In the standard (complete) slice filtration of Hill–Hopkins–Ravenel [HHR16, Definition 4.1], the slice cells are $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H}$ and $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H - 1}$ for all $H \leq G$. The **$\mathcal{O}$-slice cells** restrict to $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$:
- $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H}$ for $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$, $n \geq 0$ (dimension $n|H|$), and
- $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H - 1}$ for $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$, $n \geq 1$ (dimension $n|H| - 1$).

When $\mathcal{O} = E_\infty$, we recover the standard slice cells. When $\mathcal{O}$ is trivial ($\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}} = \{e\}$), the cells are $S^n$ and $S^{n-1}$, recovering the Postnikov filtration.

**$\mathcal{O}$-slice connectivity.** $X \in \mathrm{Sp}^G_{\mathcal{O}}$ is **$\mathcal{O}$-slice $\geq n$** if $[\hat{S}, \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^G = 0$ for every $\mathcal{O}$-slice cell $\hat{S}$ of dimension $< n$.

**$\mathcal{O}$-slice filtration.** The tower of localizations $\cdots \to \tau^{\mathcal{O}}_{\geq n+1} X \to \tau^{\mathcal{O}}_{\geq n} X \to \cdots$ is obtained by Bousfield localization with respect to the $\mathcal{O}$-slice cells. Each cell $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H}$ is compact in $\mathrm{Sp}^G$; since $\iota_{\mathcal{O}}$ admits a right adjoint $r_{\mathcal{O}}$ ([BH19, Proposition 4.8]), the objects $r_{\mathcal{O}}(G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H})$ are compact in $\mathrm{Sp}^G_{\mathcal{O}}$, and the localizations exist by [HHR16, §4.2].

---

## Proof

Both directions use the **isotropy separation cofiber sequence**. For $H \leq G$ and the family $\mathcal{P}$ of proper subgroups of $H$, the classifying space $E\mathcal{P}$ satisfies $E\mathcal{P}^H = \emptyset$ and $E\mathcal{P}^K \simeq *$ for $K \in \mathcal{P}$ [HHR16, §3.3]. Writing $\widetilde{E\mathcal{P}}$ for the cofiber of $E\mathcal{P}_+ \to S^0$, the geometric fixed point functor is $\Phi^H(-) = (\widetilde{E\mathcal{P}} \wedge -)^H$. Since $\Phi^H$ is symmetric monoidal and $(\rho_H)^H \cong \mathbb{R}$, we have $\Phi^H(S^{k\rho_H}) \simeq S^k$ and consequently
$$[S^{k\rho_H}, \widetilde{E\mathcal{P}} \wedge Y]^H \cong \pi_k(\Phi^H Y) \tag{$\ast$}$$
for any $H$-spectrum $Y$.

### Forward Direction ($\Rightarrow$)

Suppose $X$ is $\mathcal{O}$-slice $\geq n$. We show $\Phi^H X$ is $(\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor - 1)$-connected for every $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$. When $\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor = 0$, the conclusion is $(-1)$-connectivity, which holds for any spectrum, so assume $\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor \geq 1$.

We proceed by **strong induction on $|H|$**.

**Base case** ($H = e$). $\Phi^e X = X^e$ and $\lfloor n/1 \rfloor = n$. The hypothesis gives $\pi_k(X^e) = 0$ for $k < n$ (since $S^k$ is an $\mathcal{O}$-slice cell of dimension $k < n$). Hence $X^e$ is $(n-1)$-connected.

**Inductive step.** Fix $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$ with $|H| > 1$ and set $m := \lfloor n/|H| \rfloor \geq 1$. Assume the result for all $\mathcal{O}$-admissible subgroups of smaller order. Write $Y := \mathrm{Res}^G_H \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X$.

For $0 \leq k \leq m - 1$, the hypothesis gives $[G/H_+ \wedge S^{k\rho_H}, \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^G = 0$ (since $k|H| \leq (m-1)|H| < n$). By the Wirthmüller isomorphism:
$$[S^{k\rho_H}, Y]^H = 0 \quad \text{for all } 0 \leq k \leq m - 1.$$

We deduce $\pi_k(\Phi^H X) = 0$ for $0 \leq k \leq m - 1$. The isotropy separation cofiber sequence $E\mathcal{P}_+ \wedge Y \to Y \to \widetilde{E\mathcal{P}} \wedge Y$ yields the long exact sequence:
$$\cdots \to [S^{k\rho_H}, Y]^H \xrightarrow{\beta_*} \pi_k(\Phi^H Y) \xrightarrow{\partial} [S^{k\rho_H - 1}, E\mathcal{P}_+ \wedge Y]^H \to \cdots$$

Since $[S^{k\rho_H}, Y]^H = 0$, exactness gives $\ker(\partial) = \mathrm{im}(\beta_*) = 0$, so $\partial$ is injective and $\pi_k(\Phi^H Y)$ injects into $[S^{k\rho_H - 1}, E\mathcal{P}_+ \wedge Y]^H$. We show this group vanishes.

The space $E\mathcal{P}$ is an $H$-CW complex built from equivariant cells $H/K_+ \wedge S^{m\rho_K}$ for proper $K \in \mathcal{P}$ and $m \geq 0$ [HHR16, §3.3]. Since $E\mathcal{P}$ is a sequential colimit of finite $H$-CW complexes, and $S^{k\rho_H - 1}$ is *compact* in the $H$-equivariant stable category, $[S^{k\rho_H - 1}, -]^H$ commutes with the resulting filtered colimit. It suffices to show the vanishing at each finite stage.

At each stage, the cofiber sequences reduce to maps out of cells $H/K_+ \wedge S^{m\rho_K}$. The Wirthmüller isomorphism gives
$$[S^{j\rho_H}, H/K_+ \wedge S^{m\rho_K} \wedge \mathrm{Res}^H_K Y]^H \cong [S^{(j[H:K] - m) \cdot \rho_K}, \mathrm{Res}^G_K \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^K$$
using $\mathrm{Res}^H_K \rho_H \cong [H:K] \cdot \rho_K$. Since $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$ and $K \leq H$, the restriction axiom gives $K \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$ (axiom 4 with $L = K$: $(e \cap K) = e \xrightarrow{\mathcal{T}} K$).

Setting $k' = j[H:K] - m$, the dimension is $k'|K| = j|H| - m|K| \leq j|H| < n$ (since $m \geq 0$). The $\mathcal{O}$-slice $\geq n$ hypothesis gives $[G/K_+ \wedge S^{k'\rho_K}, \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^G = 0$, and by Wirthmüller, $[S^{k'\rho_K}, \mathrm{Res}^G_K \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^K = 0$. The same argument applies to odd cells: replacing $j\rho_H$ by $j\rho_H - 1$, the Wirthmüller transformation gives $[S^{(j[H:K]-m)\rho_K - 1}, \mathrm{Res}^G_K \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^K$, a map from an odd $\mathcal{O}$-slice cell of dimension $j|H| - m|K| - 1 \leq j|H| - 1 < n$, hence vanishes by hypothesis.

Therefore $\pi_k(\Phi^H Y) = 0$ for $0 \leq k \leq m - 1$, and $\Phi^H X$ is $(\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor - 1)$-connected.

---

### Reverse Direction ($\Leftarrow$)

Suppose $\Phi^H X$ is $(\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor - 1)$-connected for every $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$. We show $X$ is $\mathcal{O}$-slice $\geq n$. By Wirthmüller, it suffices to show:
- $[S^{k\rho_H}, \mathrm{Res}^G_H \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^H = 0$ for all $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$, $k \geq 0$ with $k|H| < n$, and
- $[S^{k\rho_H - 1}, \mathrm{Res}^G_H \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^H = 0$ for all $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$, $k \geq 1$ with $k|H| - 1 < n$.

We proceed by **strong induction on $|H|$**. Write $Y := \mathrm{Res}^G_H \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X$.

**Base case** ($H = e$). $\rho_e = \mathbb{R}$, so $S^{k\rho_e} = S^k$. The hypothesis gives $\pi_k(X^e) = 0$ for $k < n$, which is the required vanishing.

**Inductive step.** Fix $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$ with $|H| > 1$, assuming the result for smaller subgroups. The isotropy separation sequence yields:
$$[S^{k\rho_H}, E\mathcal{P}_+ \wedge Y]^H \xrightarrow{\alpha_*} [S^{k\rho_H}, Y]^H \xrightarrow{\beta_*} [S^{k\rho_H}, \widetilde{E\mathcal{P}} \wedge Y]^H.$$

**Right-hand term.** By $(\ast)$, $[S^{k\rho_H}, \widetilde{E\mathcal{P}} \wedge Y]^H \cong \pi_k(\Phi^H X)$. Since $\Phi^H X$ is $(\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor - 1)$-connected and $k|H| < n$ implies $k \leq \lfloor n/|H| \rfloor - 1$, this vanishes.

**Left-hand term.** As in the forward direction, $E\mathcal{P}$ is a sequential colimit of finite $H$-CW complexes with equivariant cells $H/K_+ \wedge S^{m\rho_K}$. Since $S^{k\rho_H}$ is compact, $[S^{k\rho_H}, -]^H$ commutes with the filtered colimit. The Wirthmüller isomorphism gives:
$$[S^{k\rho_H}, H/K_+ \wedge S^{m\rho_K} \wedge \mathrm{Res}^H_K Y]^H \cong [S^{(k[H:K]-m) \cdot \rho_K}, \mathrm{Res}^G_K \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^K.$$

Since $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$ and $K \leq H$, the restriction axiom gives $K \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$. Setting $k' = k[H:K] - m$, the dimension is $k'|K| = k|H| - m|K| \leq k|H| < n$. Since $|K| < |H|$, the inductive hypothesis gives $[S^{k'\rho_K}, \mathrm{Res}^G_K \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^K = 0$.

**Combining.** Both terms vanish, so $[S^{k\rho_H}, Y]^H = 0$ for $k|H| < n$.

**Odd-dimensional cells.** Apply $[S^{k\rho_H - 1}, -]^H$ to the isotropy separation sequence:
$$[S^{k\rho_H - 1}, E\mathcal{P}_+ \wedge Y]^H \to [S^{k\rho_H - 1}, Y]^H \to [S^{k\rho_H - 1}, \widetilde{E\mathcal{P}} \wedge Y]^H.$$

For the right-hand term: since $\Phi^H$ is symmetric monoidal and $\Phi^H(S^{\rho_H}) \simeq S^1$, we have $\Phi^H(S^{k\rho_H - 1}) \simeq \Phi^H(S^{k\rho_H}) \wedge \Phi^H(S^{-1}) \simeq S^k \wedge S^{-1} \simeq S^{k-1}$ (valid for $k \geq 1$ in the stable category). Therefore $[S^{k\rho_H - 1}, \widetilde{E\mathcal{P}} \wedge Y]^H \cong \pi_{k-1}(\Phi^H X)$. Since $\Phi^H X$ is $(\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor - 1)$-connected, this vanishes for $k - 1 \leq \lfloor n/|H| \rfloor - 1$, i.e., $k \leq \lfloor n/|H| \rfloor$, which is the range $k|H| - 1 < n$. (Note: when $k = 1$, we need $\pi_0(\Phi^H X) = 0$, which requires $\lfloor n/|H| \rfloor \geq 1$; this holds since $k|H| - 1 < n$ with $k = 1$ gives $|H| \leq n$.)

For the left-hand term: the same compactness and CW-filtration argument (now with $S^{k\rho_H - 1}$ compact) reduces to maps involving equivariant cells $H/K_+ \wedge S^{m\rho_K}$. The Wirthmüller isomorphism gives
$$[S^{k\rho_H - 1}, H/K_+ \wedge S^{m\rho_K} \wedge \mathrm{Res}^H_K Y]^H \cong [S^{(k[H:K]-m) \cdot \rho_K - 1}, \mathrm{Res}^G_K \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^K.$$
This is a map from an odd $K$-slice cell of dimension $(k[H:K]-m)|K| - 1 = k|H| - m|K| - 1 \leq k|H| - 1 < n$. Since $K \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$ and $|K| < |H|$, the inductive hypothesis gives the vanishing.

Combining even and odd cases: $[\hat{S}, \iota_{\mathcal{O}} X]^G = 0$ for all $\mathcal{O}$-slice cells $\hat{S}$ of dimension $< n$. Hence $X$ is $\mathcal{O}$-slice $\geq n$. $\square$

---

## Remarks

**Monotonicity in $\mathcal{O}$.** If $\mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{O}'$ corresponds to $\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathcal{T}'$, then $\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}} \subseteq \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}'}$ and the $\mathcal{O}$-slice filtration is coarser than the $\mathcal{O}'$-slice filtration. In particular, $\mathcal{O}'$-slice $\geq n$ implies $\mathcal{O}$-slice $\geq n$.

**Regular slice variant.** Ullman [Ull13, §3] introduced the regular slice filtration using only cells $G/H_+ \wedge S^{n\rho_H}$. The $\mathcal{O}$-regular variant satisfies: $X$ is $\mathcal{O}$-regular slice $\geq n$ iff $\Phi^H X$ is $(\lceil n/|H| \rceil - 1)$-connected for all $H \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}}$.

**The case $G = C_{p^n}$.** Transfer systems are in bijection with downward-closed subsets of $\{C_1, C_p, \ldots, C_{p^n}\}$. The theorem gives a family of slice filtrations indexed by these subsets, interpolating between Postnikov ($\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}} = \{C_1\}$) and HHR ($\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{O}} = \mathrm{Sub}(G)$).
